Graphical abstract A new trinuclear CdII-coordination polymer based on in situ generated 5-nitrobenzene-1,2,3- tricarboxylate (nbtc3-) ligand exhibits a previously unknown 2D (3,4,9)-connected (3.42)(32.43.5) (36.411.510.68.8) topology as well as 3D H-bonding pillared architecture. A new CdII-coordination polymer, namely, [Cd3(nbtc)2·4H2O] n (1), has been hydrothermally synthesized with in situ generated 5-nitrobenzene-1,2,3-tricarboxylate (nbtc3−) ligand. Single-crystal X-ray diffraction study reveals that compound 1 is constructed from trinuclear CdII clusters and exhibits a previously unknown 2D (3,4,9)-connected (3.42)(32.43.5)(36.411.510.68.8) topology as well as 3D H-bonding pillared architecture. In addition, the photoluminescent property and thermal stability of 1 were also discussed. Keywords CdII-coordination polymer In situ ligand synthesis Network topology Luminescence Coordination polymers, also featuring as metal–organic frameworks (MOFs), have attracted intense attention for their potential applications [1]. Much work has been devoted to the rational design of MOFs with specific structures and topologies. In this context, the network topological approach not only serves as a powerful tool for simplifying the structure of complicated crystalline compound, but also plays a pivotal role in the rational design of certain functional materials with desirable properties [2,3]. So far, a variety of uninodal and binodal network topologies that are based on 3-, 4-, 6-connected and bor, pto, ctn, rtl, pyr, cor topologies have been realized [2–4], and also containing some MOFs with highly connected structures, such as the uninodal 7-, 8-, 9-, 10-, 11-, 12-connected frameworks [4,5] and the binodal (3,8)-, (3,9)-, (3,10)-, (3,12)-, (4,8)-, (4,10)-, and (6,8)-connected frameworks [6,7]. Undoubtedly, above-mentioned significative work enlarges our scope of knowledge and offers some new way for further exploratory synthesis of novel extended crystalline solid-state materials. However, even now, there is unfavorable lack of systematic and characteristic investigation on multimodal highly connected frameworks, for example, (3,4,7)-, (3,4,9)-, (4,5,12)- and (4,8,12)-connected frameworks, which are considered to be difficult to achieve [8]. In this regard, one seductive avenue for constructing highly connected networks is to use polynuclear metal clusters as nodes, because they possess larger sizes, more outward-connecting sites, and smaller steric hindrance for the organic linkers. As a result, multidentate organic ligands with suitable metal salts have been proved to be advantageous for building such frameworks [9]. Based on above analysis, and inspired by the delightful results on the MOFs constructed from rigid 3,3′,5,5′-azobenzenetetracarboxylic acid [10,11], herein, we firstly choose a new multidentate carboxylate ligand, 3,3′,4,4′,5,5′-azobenzenehexacarboxylic acid (H6abhca), to generate multimodal highly connected MOFs. Surprisingly, when the hydrothermal reaction of Cd(NO3)2·6H2O, H6abhca and NaOH was carried out [12], we unexpectedly obtained the compound [Cd3(nbtc)2·4H2O] n (1) with the in situ generated 5-nitro-benzene-1,2,3-tricarboxylate (nbtc3−), which shows a unique (3,4,9)-connected 2D net topology based on trinuclear CdII clusters. In this communication, we present the crystal structure of 1, the coordination modes of carboxylate ligand, along with its topological analysis on the ultimate framework. Moreover, the luminescent property and thermal stability of 1 were also investigated. The single-crystal X-ray diffraction analysis [13] reveals that compound 1 crystallizes in the monoclinic system, space group P21/c (No. 14) (Supplementary material, Table S1). And its asymmetric unit is composed of three crystallographically independent CdII centers, two types of separate nbtc3− (L-I and L-II) ligands as well as four coordination H2O molecules (Fig. 1 ). The CdII atom is coordinated by nbtc3− ligands, rather than the deprotonated species of H6abhca. And thus, this means that there is the in situ transformation from H6abhca to nbtc3 − occurred under the hydrothermal conditions, since no H3nbtc ligand was used in the reaction (Supplementary material, Scheme S1). As depicted in Fig. S1 (see Supplementary material), the Cd1 and Cd2 centers both are eight-coordinated by eight oxygen atoms coming from two L-I, two L-II and two water ligands (Supplementary material, Fig. S1a and S1b), resulting in a triangular dodecahedral coordination geometry; while the Cd3 is ligated to seven oxygen atoms from two L-I, two L-II and two aqua ligands, completing its highly distorted pentagonal bipyramidal configuration (Supplementary material, Fig. S1c). The Cd–O bond distances are in the range of 2.260(5)–2.776(6)Å for Cd–Ocarboxylic and 2.279(6)–2.678(5)Å for Cd–Owater, which are well comparable to those reported for other cadmium–oxygen donor compounds (Supplementary material, Table S2) [6b,14], and the O–Cd–O bond angles fall in the range of 53.95(18)–156.5(2)° (Supplementary material, Table S3). Remarkably, despite the rigid skeleton of nbtc3− ligand, its carboxylate groups exhibit changeable and flexible self-modulation coordination modes yet. As shown in Schemes S2 and S3 (see Supplementary material), the L-I ligand adopts 6-connected mode (I) to connect six Cd(II) ions with trans–syn bridging (A), μ–O, O′syn–μ–O′, O′ (B) and η–O, O′–μ–O′, O′ (C) bridging coordination modes, respectively; while the L-II ligand also links six Cd(II) ions (II) using the η–O, O′–μ–O′, O′(C), μ–O,O–μ–O, O′–μ–O′, O′ (D) and μ–O′, O′ (E) bridging coordination modes, respectively. The bridging coordination fashion C, notably, is both present in two independent nbtc3− ligands, due to its preferential and stable chelated and bridging configuration [15]. As a consequence, three crystallographically independent Cd(II) centers are linked by L ligands with these coordination modes into a triangular trinuclear CdII clusters (Fig. 2 a) with Cd···Cd distances in the range of 3.826(1)–4.747(1)Å. As shown in Fig. 2b, these trinuclear CdII clusters are further connected by oxygen atoms of carboxylate groups (O1, O13, O14 from L-I ligands and O7, O11, O12 from L-II ligands) and bridging coordinated water molecules (O17 and O20) to form an infinite rod-shaped secondary building units (SBUs) along c-axis. In such SBUs, the trinuclear CdII clusters are arranged in nonlinear fashion with intercluster center distance of 6.198Å. Moreover, through two carboxylate oxygen atoms of L-II ligands (O2 from the D-carboxylate groups of L-II ligands, O8 from E-carboxylate groups of another L-II ligands), these rod-shaped SBUs link their adjacent inverse directional rod-shaped SBUs into an extended 2D layers with protrudent rigid arms at both side along the bc plane (Fig. 2c). In addition, these armed layers are further arranged to construct a 3D H-bonding pillared edifice via R 2 2(18) and R 2 2(22) H-binding rings (Supplementary material, Figs. S2, S3), which occurs between oxygen atoms (O9, O6) of nitro groups from nbtc3− ligands and the coordinated water ligands (O18, O19) from adjacent layers (Supplementary material, Table S4). In order to identify the complicated connectivity of the ligands and metal centers, the network topology of 1 is further analyzed. In 1, each L-I ligand links four trinuclear CdII clusters and serves as a 4-connected node, and each L-II ligand bridges three trinuclear CdII clusters and acts as a 3-connected node; while each trinuclear CdII cluster separately joins four L-I ligands, three L-II ligands and another two trinuclear CdII clusters through aqua ligands, thus, it can be simplified as a 9-connected node. Notably, the aqua ligand serves as a 2-connecting spacer between the trinuclear CdII clusters. As a whole, the topology of the resulting 2D network can be described as a unique trinodal (3, 4, 9)-connected net with the Schläli symbol of (3.42)(32.43.5)(36.411.510.68.8) analyzed by OLEX program [16] (Fig. 3 ). As far as we know, although some highly connected coordination networks, which are based on f-block metal ions, ploynuclear units and multicarboxylate ligands with diverse connectivity, have been demonstrated [8,17,18], the net in 1 represents a novel mutilnodal highly connected 2D topology structure [19]. For the PXRD patterns of 1 (Supplementary material, Fig. S4), although the experimental patterns have a few unindexed diffraction peaks and some are slightly broadened in comparison with those simulated from the single-crystal data, it can still be regarded that the bulk as-synthesized materials represent the pure phase of 1. The thermostability of 1 was studied by thermogravimetric analyses (TGA) (Supplementary material, Fig. S5). TGA curve for 1 shows that the first weight loss before 215°C corresponds to the release of the coordination water (obsd. 7.78%, calcd. 7.89%). Whereafter, the framework collapsed in the temperature range of 328–420°C, during which the organic groups were burnt. The remaining weight may be attributed to the formation of CdO (obsd. 42.5%, calcd. 42.17%). The solid-state luminescent spectra of 1 and free H3nbtc ligand have been studied at room temperature (Supplementary material, Fig. S6). It was observed that the emission peak at 373.2nm (λ ex =310nm) for free H3nbtc ligand, which may be attributable to π∗→n or π∗→π internal transitions of the ligand [20]. In comparison with that of the free ligands, a remarkable hypochromatic shift occurs in the maximum emission peak in 1 (the mission maxima at 448nm upon excitation of 315nm). However, since the CdII ions are difficult to oxidize or reduce, these bands should be assigned to the intraligand fluorescent emissions [21] that are tuned by the metal–ligand interactions and deprotonated effect of the tricarboxyl ligands. In conclusion, the trinuclear CdII-compound has been obtained by in situ generation of 5-nitrobenzene-1,2,3-tricarboxylate (nbtc3−) from 3,3′,4,4′,5,5′-azobenzenehexacarboxylic acid (H6abhca) precursor under hydrothermal conditions. Interestingly, 1 exhibits a new (3,4,9)-connected coordination network with (3.42)(32.43.5)(36.411.510.68.8) topology, which will significantly enrich the relevant knowledge of crystal engineering for coordination solids. This work also reveals that such anionic tecton (nbtc3− or abhca6−) may potentially act as an effective linker for the design and construction of highly connected coordination frameworks, and further efforts on this perspective are under way.
