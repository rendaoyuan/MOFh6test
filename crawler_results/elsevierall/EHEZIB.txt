A novel coordination polymer, {[Eu2(L)3(CH3OH)(H2O)]·(CH3OH)(H2O)}n ( 1 ), has been synthesized by solvothermal reaction of Eu(NO3)3·6H2O and 9,9-diethylfluorene-2,7-dicarboxylic acid in a mixture solution of CH3OH and H2O. Single crystal X-ray structural analysis reveals that complex 1 crystallizes in an orthorhombic, space group P2(1)2(1)2(1), holding the unusual (2,3,8)-connected 3D topological network. Thermogravimetric analysis shows remarkable thermal stability of the structural framework of compound 1 , the photoluminescence properties are also discussed. Graphical Abstract The novel 3D coordination polymers, {[Eu2(L)3(CH3OH)(H2O)]·(CH3OH)(H2O)}n (1), constructed from Eu(III) ions and 9,9-diethylfluorene-2,7-dicarboxylate with remarkable thermal stability and strong fluorescence emission in the solid state. Keywords Fluorene derivatives Coordination polymer Topological structure Photoluminescence A fluorene and its derivatives, important components (1.0–2.0%) in high-temperature tar of the petrochemicals as the effective precursor in the biosynthesis and industrial synthesis [1,2], play a unique role in photoluminescent and electroluminescent materials for recent decades and have drawn much attention of materials chemists and devices physicists [3–6]. Fluorene derivatives have been the star-molecules in the organic luminescent materials as well as one of the research focus and forefronts in the optoelectronic information materials, which have rigid biphenyl aromatic moieties with a wide energy gap (>2.90eV) in the backbones, high thermal stability and fine luminescent efficiency [7]. It's a typical case that the polyfluorene derivatives exhibit fine blue-emitting color in recent investigations [8,9]. Currently, there still remain many crucial problems in the fluorene-based luminescent materials because of the immature light-emitting mechanisms or theories. For example, how to affect energy levels of the materials from diverse chromophore groups or conjugate units [10]. According to the spin conservation principle of quantum chemistry, the luminescent efficiency would be enhanced through the spin–orbit coupling effect between metal ions and organic ligands by the introduction of some metal ions into the luminescent materials [11–13]. A key step for construction of the conjugated organic ligands and metal ion (d–π, or f–π) resultants is to design suitable bridging connectors that can promote the energy transfer from the π-block antenna chromophores to the centers [14,15]. From the viewpoint of synthetic and coordination chemistry, the fluorenes are one of the most interesting precursors, of which active hydrogen atoms on 2, 7 and 9 positions are easily replaced by coordination functional groups or chromophore groups for connection of metal ions [16–19]. It has been proven that 2,7-dicarboxyl fluorenes are versatile ligands for fabrication of inorganic–organic hybrid coordination polymers, of which carboxylates can assume different coordination conformations from terminal monodentate to various bridging modes to yield metal–oxygen polynuclear clusters, chains or three-dimensional frameworks, and the strong coordinating ability of carboxylates can bring high thermal stabilities of the materials [20,21]. On the other hand, modifications of the size and shape of chromophore groups in 9,9-position of fluorenes have also a great effect on the topological structures or properties of the resulting complexes. However, previous studies on the coordination chemistry of fluorene derivatives mainly focused on transition metals, and lanthanide compounds have not been reported up to now. In this paper we will report synthesis, and characterizations of a new europium complex based on 9,9-diethylfluorene-2,7-dicarboxylic acid (L). Complex 1 was obtained from the solvothermal reaction of Eu(NO3)3·6H2O and 9,9-diethylfluorene-2,7-dicarboxylic acid in methanol and H2O [22], which is formulated as {[Eu2(L)3(CH3OH)(H2O)]·(CH3OH)(H2O)}n [23]. Single crystal X-ray diffraction analysis reveals that complex 1 holds a three-dimensional framework with microporous tunnels lodged by free methanol and water molecules. Two kinds of linking manners of the organic ligands in 1 are shown: (a) as μ 3-bridging to link three europium ions, in which one carboxylate adopts a bidentate chelate mode coordinating to one Eu(III) ion and the other adopts a bidentate chelating-bridging mode connecting two Eu(III) ions (Fig. 1a); and (b) as μ 4-bridging to link four Eu(III) ions, in which one carboxylate adopts a bidentate bridging mode linking two Eu(III) ions, and the other adopts a bidentate chelating-bridging mode connecting two Eu(III) ions (Fig. 1b). Eu1 and Eu2 exhibit different coordination environments in the asymmetric unit of the complex: Eu1 is coordinated by eight oxygen atoms of the carboxylates, and Eu2 is connected by nine oxygen atoms, of which seven oxygen atoms are from the carboxylates, one from coordination water molecule and the other one from the methanol (Fig. 1c). Binuclear cluster [Eu2(L)3(CH3OH)(H2O)] formed by carboxylates, can be viewed as a basic building block for the whole structure with the near Eu1–Eu2 distance being 3.804Å. Each two of such units are connected by the carboxylates as bidentate bridging to give rise to a 1D infinite chain, which is further jointed by other carboxylates of the dicarboxylate-fluorene ligands along different directions to form a final three-dimensional framework with microporous tunnels (Fig. 1d). Remarkably, the framework of 1 exhibits interesting topological structure. Since the dicarboxylate-fluorene ligands can be considered as two-, three-connected and binuclear cluster as 8-connected, complex 1 displays the (2,3,8)-connected two-nodal three-dimensional topology (Fig. 2 ), of which the net can be described with the Schläfli symbol of (316; 427; 520; 63)(38; 411; 58; 6) [24]. The vertex symbol analysis was calculated by TOPOS4 [25]. Thermogravimetic analysis (TGA) of 1 was performed under a flow of nitrogen gas from room temperature to 800°C at a heating rate of 10°C/min. The first weight loss of 7.81% (calcd: 7.53%) corresponds to two water and two methanol molecules per formula unit {[Eu2(L)3(CH3OH)(H2O)]·(CH3OH)(H2O)}n from 130 to 200°C, hardly weight loss from 200 to 400°C, and then lose mainly weight upon increasing temperature. Differential thermal analysis (DTA) studies show a strong absorption heat peak from 400 to 550°C in the company of the phase change, which indicates the collapse of the framework of compound 1 (Fig. S1). The phase purity of 1 was confirmed by the powder X-ray diffraction (XRD, using a Bruker D8 Focus X-ray diffractometer equipped with Cu Kα radiation) patterns of the bulk sample, which is consistent with the calculated results (Fig. 3 ). The solid-state photoluminescent spectra for 1 and the ligand of 9,9-diethylfluorene-2,7-dicarboxylic acid, were measured (Fig. 4 ). When excited at 315nm under ambient temperature, the ligand of 9,9-diethylfluorene-2,7-dicarboxylic acid shows strong broad emission peak with the maximum wavelength centered at ca. 381nm, which may originate from the ligand-to-ligand πL →πL* charge transfer (LLCT) transition emission; the strong broad peaks at 424nm and weak peak at 484 and 527nm for complex 1 may be assigned to ligand-to-metal or metal-to-ligand charge transfer (LMCT or MLCT) transition emission [26]. When excited at 250nm, complex 1 exhibits the strong emission peaks at 612nm attributed to 5Do → 7F2 energy transition, which is consistent with the known characteristic emission peaks of Eu(III) ions [27]; and other character emissive peaks of the Eu(III) ions have not been shown, which may be sheltered upon the affection of the organic ligand. The fluorescence of the complex above may be the result of the coordination interactions and crystal packing interactions in the solid state by radiationless decay of the intra-ligand or lanthanide ion emission excited state. More detailed theoretical and spectroscopic studies are in progress for a better understanding of the luminescent mechanism. The UV–Vis absorption can probably be assigned to intra-ligand (π–π*) charge transfer because almost similar absorption peaks are observed for the free ligand and compound 1 (Fig. S2 in the Supplementary materials). In summary, a novel three-dimensional framework of Eu(III) complex was synthesized under solvothermal condition. Structural analysis of compound 1 displays the unusual (2,3,8)-connected topological network; the luminescent measurement suggests an intense near blue emission at solid state; TGA analysis shows high thermal stability of the framework for 1 . This work may provide useful information for employing fluorene-based multicarboxylate ligands to assemble new functional materials with novel topological networks.
