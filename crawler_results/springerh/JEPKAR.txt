Manuscript Journal of Chemical Crystallography, Vol. 36, No. 5, May 2006 ( C⃝2006) DOI: 10.1007/s10870-005-9066-0 Synthesis and crystal structure of catena-poly[aqua(cis-syn-cis-dicyclohexyl-18-crown-6) tetra(thiocyanato)barium(II) platinum(II)]: [BaPt(NCS)4(C20H36O6)(H2O)]n Yun-Ming Sun,(1,2) Feng-Ying Dong,(1,2) Jian-Min Dou,(1)∗ Da-Cheng Li,(1) Xi-Ke Gao,(1) and Da-Qi Wang(1) Received September 22, 2005; accepted December 20, 2005 Published Online February 8, 2006 An organic–inorganic hybrid polymeric complex, [BaPt(NCS)4(C20H36O6)(H2O)]n or [[Ba(DC18C6-A)(H2O)][Pt(SCN)4]]n (1) (DC18C6-A = cis-syn-cis-dicyclohexyl-18- crown-6) has been synthesized by the reaction of cis-syn-cis-dicyclohexyl-18-crown-6 with Ba(SCN)2, BaCO3 and H2PtCl4, and characterized by elementary analysis, FT-IR spectra and X-ray single-crystal diffraction. It crystallizes as monoclinic, space group P 21/n with crystallographic data: a = 14.275(6) ˚A, b = 13.165(5) ˚A, c = 18.971(8) ˚A, β = 106.294(6)◦, V = 3422(2) ˚A3, Z = 4, Dcalcd = 1.854 g/cm3, F(000) = 1856, R1 = 0.0491, wR2 = 0.0709. The asymmetric unit of complex 1 contains one [Ba(DC18C6-A)(H2O)]2+ complex cation and one [Pt(SCN)4]2−complex anion, which are assembled into an inﬁnite polymeric chain via the bridging SCN groups. The coordinated water molecule gives two different intrachain and interchain hydrogen bonds. KEY WORDS: Synthesis and crystal structure; coordination polymer; platinum complex; cis-syn-cis- dicyclohexyl-18-crown-6. Experimental cis-syn-cis-Dicyclohexyl-18-crown-6 was separated and puriﬁed using the method as reported by Izatt et al.16 All chemicals were commercially available and used without further puriﬁcation. The microanalytical data were obtained from a Perkin-Elmer 2400 II analyser. FT-IR spectra of the complex were measured on a Nicolet-460 FT-IR spectrometer in the range 4000–400cm−1 as KBr pellets. Synthesis of complex 1 This complex was prepared by adding 10 mL aqueous mixture of BaCO3 (0.0494 g, 0.25 mmol), H2PtCl4 (0.25 mmol) and Ba(SCN)2 (0.759 g, 3 mmol) to 10 mL cis-syn-cis- dicyclohexyl-18-crown-6 (0.3722 g, 1 mmol) in 1,2-dichloroethane. The reaction mixture was stirred for 3 h at room temperature, then the organic phase was separated. Yellow crys- tals were obtained from 4:1 diethyl ether/1,2- dichloroethane solution over a period of 15 days. Yield, 60% (based on Pt). m.p. 166– 167 ◦C. Anal. calcd for C24H38N4BaPtO7S4: C, 30.17; H, 4.01; N, 5.87. found: C, 30.11; H, 3.92; N, 5.94. The bond lengths of Pt–S, S–C and C≡N are in the ranges 2.302(2)–2.333(2), 1.655(10)–1.674(9) and 1.129(9)–1.148(9) ˚A, which are basically consistent with the corresponding values in com- plex [K(DB18C6)]2[Pt(SCN)4].19 For [Ba(DC18C6-A)(H2O)]2+ complex cation, six O atoms of crown ether molecule adopt the boat conformation. Four of them, adjacent to the cyclohexyl rings, serve as the bottom of “boat” and form a plane with the Ba atom lying about 0.365(2) ˚A out of it. O(3) and O(6) give the deviations of 0.725(2) and 0.260(2) ˚A from the plane. The Ba atom lies in the crown ether molecule and is coordinated by nine atoms, six O atoms of a crown ether, one O atom from a water molecule and two N atoms from two [Pt(SCN)4]2−complex anions. The bond lengths of Ba–O(crown ether) are in the range 2.740(6)–2.856(5) ˚A, which is close to that found in complex [Ba(DC18C6- A)(H2O)](SCN)2.20 The distances of Ba(1)–N(1) and Ba(1)–N(3)#1 are 2.797(8) and 2.946(7) ˚A, which are shorter than the sum of the van der Waals radii of N atom (1.55 ˚A) and the effective ionic radii of nine-coordinated Ba2+ ion (1.61 ˚A),21, 22 indicating strong Ba–N interactions. The N(1)–Ba(1)–N(3)#1 bond angle is 145.5(2)◦. As shown in Fig. 2, the [Ba(DC18C6-A)(H2O)]2+ complex cations and [Pt(SCN)4]2−complex anions are assem- bled into an inﬁnite chain via bridge SCN ligands. The coordinated water molecule forms two different hydrogen bonds, namely an intrachain O–H···S and an interchain O–H···N hydrogen bond (Table 3). Thus, two adjacent chains are dimerized by means of O–H···N hydrogen bond (Fig. 3). A similar self-assembly mode also occurs in the recently reported complex [PbPd(NCS)4(C20H36O6)(H2O)]n.23 According to the search in CSD Vesion 5.26,24 there are more than 100 complexes containing crown ether-Ba2+ segment. How- ever, none of them involves transition metal inorganic building blocks. To our best knowl- edge, complex 1 may be considered as the ﬁrst structurally characterized coordination polymer, which is engrafted on the crown ether–Ba2+ segment by transition metal inorganic building blocks.
