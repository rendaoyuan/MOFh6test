As revealed by single-crystal X-ray diffraction studies, the sandwich-shaped clusters of [Cd(Mo6P4)2] are intercon- nectedbyadditional[Mg(Hen)]unitsand[Cd(H2O)]fragmentsto form an extended three-dimensional supramolecular frame- work with channels occupied by ethylenediamine molecules, protonated ethylenediamine cations and water molecules. Luminescent measurement of the title compound exhibits intensive blue and greenish-blue radiation emission upon excitation with ultraviolet light. Keywords Inorganic–organic hybrid material  Sandwich-shaped cluster  Synthesis  Structure  Luminescence 1 Introduction Among the organic–inorganic hybrid materials reported, polyoxometalates (POM) have been attracting extensive interest in solid-state materials chemistry because of their manifold potential applications in catalysis, photochemis- try, electrochromism, magnetism, medicine and diversity of their structures [1–4]. The important developments of POM chemistry are exactly studies on synthesis, structure and functional properties of POM with extended structures based on discrete metal–oxygen cluster units covalently linked by transition metal complex fragments, which can serve as bridging ligands for phosphomolybdate frame- works via covalent bonds [5]. Acting as one phosphomo- lybdate family, reduced Mo(V)/phosphate compounds are very stable structural units and often exist as poly- (oxomolybdophosphate) anions [P4Mo6] [6]. Similarly, the development of phosphomolybdate chemistry has focused on the assembly of poly-(oxomolybdophosphate) anions into larger clusters or into 1D, 2D, and 3D extended frameworks with desirable properties. For example, alkali metal, Na [7, 8], and d-block transition metal elements, Mn [9, 10], Ni [11], Fe [12] and Co [13–15], have been widely used as linker atoms to bridge-cluster anions. To our knowledge, the 3D framework of molybdenum(V) phosphates, which are constructed from Cd[P4Mo6]2 dimers bonded together with [Cd(H2O)]2 and [Mg(Hen)]2 units, has not been reported and would represent the ﬁrst observation that the poly-(oxomolybdophosphate) anions, e.g., [Mo6P4], can be modiﬁed by transition metal cations, and then cova- lently bonded to the extended framework solids by both alkali earth metals and transition metal cations. In this paper, we wish to report the hydrothermal synthesis, structural characterization and ﬂuorescent properties of a novel organic–inorganic hybrid molybdenum(V) phosphate. Elemental analyses (C, H, N and Cl) were performed on a Perkin-Elmer 2400 Series II CHNS/O elemental analyzer. Cd, Mg, Mo and P were determined by Inductively Coupled Plasma (ICP) analysis performed on a Perkin-Elmer Optima 3300DV spectrom- eter. FT-IR spectra were recorded from 400 to 4000 cm-1 on a Perkin-Elmer FT-IR spectrometer using KBr pellets. Thermogravimetric analyses were performed on a Perkin- Elmer Diamond TG/DTA instrument in ﬂowing air with a heating rate of 10 C/min. X-ray powder diffraction of the samples were recorded at room temperature on an X-ray diffractometer (Rigaku D/Max 2200PC) with a graphite monochromator and Cu Ka radiation while the voltage and electric current were held at 40 kV and 20 mA. Excitation and emission spectra were obtained using a Perkin-Elmer LS-55 instrument. 2.2 Crystal Growth Synthesis of the title compound 1: A mixture of 2 mmol (NH4)6Mo7O246H2O, 2 mmol Cd(CH3COO)22H2O, 2 mmol MgCl26H2O, 5 mmol H3PO4, 2 mmol ethylenediamine and 20 mL water was heated at180 C under static conditions for 36 h in a Teﬂon-lined stainless steel autoclave (ﬁlling vol- ume of 70%). After cooling the reaction mixture to room temperature, the brown block-like crystals were separated and washed with distilled water and ethanol many times. Finally, the product was dried in air at room temperature. 2.3 Crystal Structure Determination and Reﬁnement A suitable single crystal with dimensions 0.48 mm 9 0.38 mm 9 0.36 mm was carefully selected under an optical microscope and glued to a thin glass ﬁber with epoxy resin. X-ray intensity data were measured at 298(2) K on a Bruker SMART APEX CCD-based diffractometer (Mo Ka radiation, k = 0.71073 A˚ ). The raw frame data for the compound were integrated into SHELX-format reﬂection ﬁles and corrected for Lorentz and polarization effects using SAINT [16]. Corrections for incident and diffracted beam absorption effects were applied using SADABS [16]. Compound 1 crystallizes in the space group P-1 as determined by the systematic absences in the intensity data, intensity statistics and the successful solution and reﬁnement of the structure. The structure was solved by a combination of direct methods and difference Fourier syntheses and reﬁned against F2 by the full matrix least-squares technique. Crystal data, data collection parameters, and reﬁnement statistics for the compound 1 are listed in Table 1. Selected bond distances and bond angles are also given (see sup- porting information Tables S1 and S2). 2.4 Composition Analysis The results of the elemental analysis show that compound 1 contains 3.67, 2.04, 4.65 and 2.11 wt% of C, H, N and Cl (Calcd. 3.88, 2.16, 4.52 and 2.29 wt%), respectively. Induc- tively Coupled Plasma (ICP) analysis indicates that compound 1 contains 7.39,0.86, 37.38 and 7.87 wt% of Cd, Mg,Moand P (Calcd. 7.26, 0.79, 37.20 and 8.01 wt%). Both CMP-1 and CMP-2 are constructed from [Mo6P4] hexamers bonded together with octahedral Cd2? (see supporting information Figs. S1 and S2). As shown in Figs. S1 and S2, there is a six-membered ring of oxo-bridged Mo atoms with alternating pattern of Mo–Mo single bonds(ca. 2.6066 A˚ for CMP-1, 2.6013 A˚ for CMP-2) and non-bonding Mo_Mo contacts (ca. 3.5309 A˚ for CMP- 1, 3.5088 A˚ for CMP-2) in each [Mo6P4]. The framework is, therefore, stabilized by three Mo–Mo single bonds formed between the six single occupied 4d1 orbitals of the particular Mo centers. Among four PO4-tetrahedra, a central unit (P–O: 1.5105–1.5616 A˚ for CMP-1, 1.5045–1.5587 A˚ for CMP-2) caps the hexamer internally and three peripheral units (P–O: 1.5145–1.5742 A˚ for CMP-1, 1.4916–1.5698 A˚ for CMP-2) bridge the non-bonding Mo–Mo fragments externally and, hence, shield and further reinforce the structure. As a result, a toothed-wheel arrangement was formed. The neighboring molybdenum octahedra in Mo6O13Cl2 and Mo6O15 are edge shared with each other (for CMP-1, O–Mo–O: 71.29–170.0, Cl-Mo–O: 94.9–170.6; for CMP- 2, O–Mo–O: 71.90–170.7); and, the neighboring phosphate tetrahedra are corner-shared with the corresponding molyb- denum octahedron (for CMP-1, O–P–O: 105.9–113.4; for CMP-2, O–P–O: 102.2–113.1), although several P–O groups are protonated as a result of coordination with Cd2? and Mg2? cation. There are three independently octahedral Cd atoms in compound 1 (Fig. 1, supporting information Figs. S1 and S2), which may be divided into two types: (i) Cd1 and Cd2, where Cd1 bridges two [Mo6P4] units via three l3-O atoms that link the metal–metal-bonded molybdenum atoms toge- ther with Cd–O bonds with lengths of 2.2751(51) A˚ 9 2, 2.2582(42) A˚ 9 2, and 2.2316(42) A˚ 9 2 to produce a centrosymmetric [Cd(Mo6P4)2] dimer. Cd2 is similar to Cd1, and the average Cd2–O bond distance is 2.2577 A˚ . (ii) Cd3 bridges four P–O groups from different [Mo6P4] moieties (i.e. one [Cd2(Mo6P4)2] unit and two [Cd1(Mo6P4)2] units) and has a terminal coordinated water molecule with the distance of Cd3–O61 2.3344(64) A˚ . Compared with Cd atoms, only one type of tetrahedral Mg site in the compound 1 was found (Fig. 2). Mg1 bridges three P–O groups from one [Cd1(Mo6P4)2] unit and two [Cd2 (Mo6P4)2] units, and has a terminal coordinated Hen cation with the distance of Mg1–N7 2.0229(110) A˚ . The average Cd3–O and Mg1–O bond distances are 2.3453 and 2.2499 A˚ , respectively. However, it is notable that two types of Cd[Mo6P4]2 clusters, indicated as CMP-1 and CMP-2, are not crystallo- graphically identical. The main difference between them is that in CMP-2 only six oxygen atoms in each [Mo6P4] entity coordinates with a Mo ion (see supporting information Fig. S2). In CMP-1, apart from ﬁve oxygen atoms, one chlorine atom also coordinates with a Mo atom (see supporting information Fig. S1). Another difference between CMP-1 and CMP-2 is their arrangement in the crystal lattice in two different directions, which are almost perpendicular to each other (Figs. 1 and 2). In the lattice, both CMP-1 and CMP-2 clusters are con- nected to two Cd3(H2O)2? units to form inﬁnite 2D layers in the bc plane with irregular windows (Fig. 3). One is surrounded by two Cd3 cations and two PO4 anions with a tunnel bore diameter of 6.0131(9) A˚ based on the Cd3–Cd3 distance (Fig. 3). The other tunnel is surrounded by two Mg1 cations and four PO4 anions with a tunnel bore diameter of 9.0676(65) A˚ based on the Mg1–Mg1 distance (supporting information Figs. S3 and S4). Both tunnels are ﬁlled with ethylenediamine molecules, protonated ethylenediamine cations and lattice water molecules. Furthermore, in the crystal structure of 1, there exists extensive hydrogen bonding among the sandwich-shaped units, Cd(H2O)2?, Mg(Hen)3? units, free ethylenediamine molecules, monoprotonated and diprotonated ethylenedia- mine cations as well as with the lattice water molecules residing in the tunnels. The N–H_O hydrogen bonds are in the range 2.719–3.286 A˚ . The O–H_O hydrogen bonds are in the range 2.293–3.178 A˚ . At the same time, hydrogen bonding interactions with N–H_Cl distance of 2.875 A˚ (N3–Cl2) are also found (Table 2). The differences in intensity may be due to the preferred ori- entation of the powder samples. 3.6 Photoluminescent Properties Inorganic–organic hybrid coordination compounds, espe- cially with d10 metal centers, have been investigated for ﬂuorescence properties owing to their potential applica- tions as luminescent materials such as light-emitting diodes (LEDs) [20–22]. Therefore, in the present study, the solid- state photoluminescence properties (excitation and emis- sion plots; Fig. 7) of 1 were measured at room temperature. It can be seen that 1 exhibits features of a broad lumines- cence emission band upon excitation with ultraviolet light. Such emission band results from several overlapping transitions located at 426, 455, 486, 530 and 571 nm upon photoexcitation at 218 nm. It is known that photoexcitation of oxygen-to-metal (O ? M) ligand-to-metal charge-transfer (LMCT) bands of POM generates an electron and a hole with d and 2p character, respectively. The return of the excited POM to the ground state competes with the redox reactions of the excited state with electron acceptors or donors [23]. Since several energy levels exist in the O ? M LMCT bands, energy transfer occurs from the O ? M LMCT excited state to these levels; this has been demonstrated previously for POM [24]. Therefore, we tentatively suggest that two emissions (at 530 and 571 nm) originate from the O ? M LMCT triplet states of [Mo6P4] hexamers, and are attrib- uted to the intra-ligand emission from the molybdenum (V) phosphate anion. At the same time, we know that the excited states of polynuclear d10 metal complexes involve metal-to-ligand charge-transfer, ligand-to-metal charge-transfer, and metal- centered to ligand-centered charge-transfer due to the presence of a wide range of bridging and ancillary ligands. As for the Mo(V) phosphate of 1, which contains a Cd(II) heteroatom, also shows a d10 metal-center emission. Con- sidering the strong r-donating properties of molybdenum phosphate anions and the formation of trinuclear Cd clus- ters, we suggest that the three strong blue emissions at 486, 455 and 426 nm upon excitation at 218 nm may be assigned to the emission of the LMCT [Mo(V) phos- phate anion ? Cd(II)]. Furthermore, it is evident that the molybdenum (V) phosphate anion displays a weak lumi- nescence at ca. 530 and 571 nm in the solid state at room temperature. X-ray crystallography shows that 1 consists of a novel three-dimensional supramolec- ular framework constructed of [Cd(Mo6P4)2] clusters and covalently bonded [Mg(Hen)] coordination complexes and [Cd(H2O)] fragments. Unusual tunnels are formed in the solid with ethylenediamine molecules, protonated ethy- lenediamine cations and water molecules located within. The successful isolation of 1 represents the ﬁrst example of [Cd(Mo6P4)2] clusters that are connected by mixed alkali earth metals and transition metals to form an extended 3D framework. In addition, the compound was assessed as a good candidate for greenish-blue-light- emitting materials.
