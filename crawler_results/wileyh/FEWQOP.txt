KGaA, Weinheim 1412 FULL PAPER Introduction The magnetic properties of 3d4f heteronuclear complexes are of great interest in molecular magnetism.[1–4] Owing to the differences between d and f electrons, including in terms of their electron clouds, coordination geometries, and coor- dination abilities to donor atoms, much effort has been paid to combining such different spin carriers to synthesize new materials that can integrate the advantages of both of them for practical applications.[5] Recently, molecular magnetic refrigeration has received great attention because it is an energy-efficient and environ- mentally friendly technique that operates on the basis of the magnetocaloric effect (MCE).[6] Large-MCE materials can be used at the liquid-helium temperature to replace the ex- pensive and rare helium-3. 0D,[7] 1D,[8] 2D,[9] and 3D coordi- nation compounds[10] have shown great potential in this field. The prerequisites for a molecular material with a large MCE include a large-spin ground state, negligible magnetic anisotropy, low-lying excited spin states, dominant ferromag- netic exchange, and a large metal/ligand mass ratio, which present quite a challenge to both chemists and materials sci- entists.[7–10] On the other hand, superparamagnetism in molecular ma- terials has also attracted great attention over the past few decades. In this field, single-molecule magnets (SMMs) are interesting discrete polynuclear clusters that are formed from coordination bonds and show magnet-type behavior. 3d4f SMMs are special in terms of their large ground state, significant anisotropy of the lanthanide ions, and moderate- intensity exchange interactions between the pure lanthanide SMMs and transition-metal SMMs, which could make them excellent candidates for generating large energy barriers for the slow relaxation of the magnetization. Various 3d4f SMMs, such as Cu2Tb2, Mn6Dy6, Mn11Dy4, and Mn2Dy2, which exhibit slow relaxation of the magnetization and/or hysteresis loops at low temperatures, have been studied.[11] Recent progress has shown that both single-ion anisotropy and exchange interactions are beneficial for blocking the magnetization,[12] which provides a new understanding of 3d4f SMMs. In this context, we were interested in obtaining such at- tractive properties in metal–organic framework (MOF) ma- terials, as a new generation of molecular crystalline materi- als that are assembled through coordination bonds between organic ligands and metal ions/clusters. The high-dimension- al structural features of MOFs can not only combine the dis- crete molecules or clusters into more-stable frameworks for potential use, but also easily tune their magnetic properties from the viewpoint of crystal engineering. During our sys- tematic investigation into the use of 3-aminopyrazine-2-car- boxylic acid as a ligand in heterometallic complexes, two new isostructural lanthanideACHTUNGTRENUNG(III)copper(II) organic frame- works with unprecedented structural motifs, namely {[Ln6Cu3L6ACHTUNGTRENUNG(m3-OH)6ACHTUNGTRENUNG(m6-O)ACHTUNGTRENUNG(m3-O)2]·12H2O}n (Gd-Cu: Ln= Gd, Dy-Cu: Ln=Dy; H2L=3-hydroxypyrazine-2-carboxylic acid), were obtained. The structures have been determined by X-ray crystallography and further supported by IR spec- troscopy, elemental analysis, powder X-ray diffraction (PXRD), and thermogravimetric analysis (TGA). These in- teresting frameworks exhibited different magnetic proper- ties depending on the anisotropy of the lanthanide spin car- riers. The L2 ion acts as a tetradentate linker in which the car- boxylate group adopts a m2-h1:h1 mode in a syn-syn fashion and connects one CuII ion to one DyIII ion. Interestingly, eight m3-O atoms connect six DyIII ions to form an unusual centrosymmetric {ODy6} octahedron with an encapsulated m6-O moiety at its center (Figure 1c) and a neighboring Dy Dy distance of 3.6401(10) . Each octahedron is surrounded by 12 ligands (Figure 1d) and is further linked by CuII ions to form a unique 3D structure (Figure 1e). Topologically, if the {OLn6} octahedron were considered as a 12-connected node and the [CuL2] moiety as a two-connected node, the framework could be described as an 4,12-c net with two non-equivalent points: {436.630}ACHTUNGTRENUNG{44.62}3, which has not previ- ously been observed in heterometallic-organic frameworks (Figure 2). ACHTUNGTRENUNG{OLn6} octahedra have been found in some interesting in- organic compounds, such as [Ln6ACHTUNGTRENUNG(m6-O)ACHTUNGTRENUNG(m3-OH)8ACHTUNGTRENUNG(H2O)24]I8 ACHTUNGTRENUNG(H2O)8 (Ln=Nd, Eu, Tb, Dy)[15] and {[Yb6ACHTUNGTRENUNG(m6-O)ACHTUNGTRENUNG(m3-OH)8 ACHTUNGTRENUNG(DMF)16ACHTUNGTRENUNG(m-CN)(CN)2]6+}n.[16] However, molecular materials that are based on {OLn6} octahedra are scarce. The forma- tion of such unusual {OLn6} octahedra requires judicious se- lection of the reaction conditions, such as the reaction tem- perature, the pH value of the solution, the molar ratio of the raw materials, and the coordination nature of the ligands (for details, see the Experimental Section). To the best of our knowledge, Gd-Cu and Dy-Cu are the first examples of using {OLn6} secondary building units (SBUs) to construct heterometallic–organic frameworks. Magnetic Properties The direct-current (DC) magnetic susceptibilities of these two compounds were measured within the temperature range 2–300 K in an applied field of 1000 Oe. For Gd-Cu, the room-temperature cMT value of 7.85 cm3mol1K match- es well with the expected value of 8.08 cm3mol1K for one free GdIII ion (S=7/2, 8S7/2, g=2) and half a free CuII ion (S=1/2, g=2). The cMT products slightly decrease until 90 K, then drop dramatically to a minimum of 2.77 cm3mol1K at 2 K (Figure 3). The susceptibility data obey the Curie–Weiss law, cM=C/ACHTUNGTRENUNG(Tq), with C= 12.25 cm3mol1K and q=5.04 K. The small negative q value indicates weak antiferromagnetic interactions. The field dependence of magnetization at 2 K displays a rapid increase at low magnetic fields and finally reaches a maxi- mum value of 6.9 Nb at 7 T without clear saturation (Figure 3). The alternating current (AC) measurement does not exhibit any out-of-phase signal, thus indicating the para- magnetic nature of Gd-Cu. Conclusions In summary, two new isostructural lanthanideACHTUNGTRENUNG(III)copper(II) organic frameworks based on octahedral {OLn6} clusters have been synthesized and their new topology has been characterized. Magnetic studies have shown that Gd-Cu is an interesting magnetic refrigerant for potential low-temper- ature applications, whilst Dy-Cu exhibits slow relaxation of the magnetization at low temperatures. Further work is un- derway to study other cluster-based 3d4f MOFs with large MCE and superparamagnetic behavior. Moreover, this work also reveals a new route to molecular magnetic materials that are based on lanthanide clusters. Experimental Section All chemicals and solvents were of reagent grade.
