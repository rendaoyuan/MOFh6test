Graphical abstract Two coordination polymers [Ni3(ttmb)2(H2O)6Cl6](H2O)3 (1) and [Ni(ttmb)2(H2O)2](NO3)2(H2O)4 (2) (ttmb=1,3,5-tri(1,2,4-triazol-1-ylmethyl)-2,4,6-trimethylbenzene) were synthesized. Compound 1 forms an undulated (3,4)-connected two-dimensional network. Each undulated two-dimensional network polycatenates other two identical networks, thus giving a rare (2D→3D) parallel polycatenation network in 1. Compound 2 constructs a two-dimensional (4,4) network. The reaction of 1,3,5-tri(1,2,4-triazol-1-ylmethyl)-2,4,6-trimethylbenzene (ttmb) with Ni(II) salts yields two coordination polymers [Ni3(ttmb)2(H2O)6Cl6](H2O)3 (1) and [Ni(ttmb)2(H2O)2](NO3)2(H2O)4 (2). The flexible ligand ttmb shows the cis,cis,cis-conformation and a tri-monodentate coordination mode in 1. Each ttmb links three Ni(II) atoms to form an undulated (3,4)-connected two-dimensional network. Each undulated two-dimensional network polycatenates other two identical networks, thus giving a rare (2D→3D) parallel polycatenation network in 1. The ttmb ligand exhibits the cis,trans,trans-conformation and a two-monodentate coordination mode in 2. Each ttmb connects two Ni(II) atoms to construct a two-dimensional (4,4) network in 2. The thermal stability of compounds 1 and 2 was studied. Keywords Polycatenation network Coordination polymer Anion role Nickel complex 1,3,5-Tri(1,2,4-triazol-1-ylmethyl)-2,4,6-trimethylbenzene Great progress in the crystal engineering has provided a wide range of metal–organic frameworks (MOFs) during the past decades for their potential application as well as the variety of architectures and topologies [1]. The anions serve more than merely balancing the charges of a cationic and play the influence on the structure of a supramolecular system through coordination bonding to the metal cations or through weak interaction with organic ligands [2]. Previously we reported that the reaction of the flexible ligand 1,4-bis(1,2,4-triazol-1-yl)butane (btb) with Cd(II) salts with different anions NO 3 - , BF 4 - , ClO 4 - and PF 6 - gave a 1D chain [Cd(btb)(H2O)2(NO3)2] n , a threefold interpenetrated diamondoid network {[Cd(btb)2(H2O)2](BF4)2} n and a threefold interpenetrated α-polonium cubic network {[Cd(btb)3](ClO4)2} n and {[Cd(btb)3](PF6)2} n [3]. Dunbar and coworkers reported that the reaction of 3,6-di(2-pyridyl)-1,2,4,5-tetrazine with first-row transition metal could yield a molecular square and a pentagon with one anion accommodated in the cavities of the polygons. In this case, anions function as templates through anion-π interactions [4]. The ligands 1,2,4-triazole and its derivatives have received a great attention because of their intriguing structures and unique properties [5–7]. Recently, we reported the crystal structures of a series of transition metal coordination polymers with flexible bis(triazole) ligands such as 1,2-bis(1,2,4-triazol-1-yl)ethane [8] 1,4-bis(1,2,4-triazol-1-ylmethyl)benzene [9] and 1,4-bis(1,2,4-triazol-1-yl)butane [10]. 1,3,5-Tri(1,2,4-triazol-1-ylmethyl)-2,4,6-trimethylbenzene (ttmb) contains three triazole rings (Scheme 1 ) and can construct interesting coordination polymers. Here we report the syntheses and crystal structures of two interesting nickel coordination polymers [Ni3(ttmb)2(H2O)6Cl6](H2O)3 (1) and [Ni(ttmb)2(H2O)2](NO3)2(H2O)4 (2) with the reaction of 1,3,5-tri(1,2,4-triazol-1-ylmethyl)-2,4,6-trimethylbenzene (ttmb) with Ni(II) salts. Coordination polymer 1 [11] was synthesized by the slow diffusion method in CH3OH/H2O medium. The crystal structure of 1 [12] shows that 1 has an undulated (3,4)-connected (Each ttmb links three Ni(II) atoms and each Ni(II) atom connects four Ni(II) atoms.) two-dimensional network. Each Ni(II) atom displays a distorted octahedral coordination geometry, coordinated by two oxygen atoms from two water molecules, two Cl− anions, and two triazole nitrogen atoms from two ttmb ligands (Fig. S1). Each ttmb ligand shows the cis,cis,cis-conformation. Each bridge ligand ttmb shows a tri-monodentate coordination mode and links three Ni(II) atoms to form a (3,4)-connected two-dimensional network (Fig. 1 ). The Schläfla symbol for 1 is 3262. The Ni⋯Ni distance separation by ttmb ligand is 7.2306(3)Å. The two-dimensional layers of 1 are strongly undulated with the total thickness of 11.0Å (Fig. 2 ). In order to minimize the big void cavities and stabilize the framework, the potential voids formed by a single two-dimensional network show incorporation of other two identical networks, thus giving a threefold parallel interpenetrating network (Fig. 3 ). This is an example of parallel interlock 2D motifs to generate 3D network (2D→3D). Compound 1 exhibits a rare polycatenated (2D→3D) network. Although the catenated simple layers are relatively less common. To the best of our knowledge, only few examples of (2D→3D) entangled structures have been observed for the polycatenating systems in parallel fashion [13]. There are hydrogen bonding interactions between the coordination water molecules (O(1)) and the triazole nitrogen atoms (N(2)), Cl− anions. These hydrogen bonding interactions further stabilize the 3D network. Compound 2 exhibits a two-dimensional (4,4) network (Fig. 4 ). The coordination geometry of the Ni(II) atom is a distorted octahedron, formed by four triazole nitrogen atoms from four ttmb ligands in the equatorial plane, and two water molecules in the axial positions (Fig. S2). All ttmb ligands show the cis,trans,trans-conformation and a two-monodentate coordination mode. One triazole group of the ttmb ligand does not coordinate to Ni(II) atom. Each Ni(II) atom is bridged by four ttmb ligands to construct a two-dimensional (4,4) network (Figs. 4 and S3). The network contains the square grids (48-membered ring), with a Ni(II) atom at each corner and a ttmb ligand at each edge connecting two Ni(II) atoms. The edge lengths are 12.0863(21)Å and the inner angles are 84.71° and 95.29°. The two-dimensional networks stack parallel along the a direction with the inter-layers distance equal to the a-axis translation (Fig. S4). There are versatile hydrogen bonding interactions between the coordination (O(4)), the lattice water molecules (O(5)) and the nitrate anions (O(2)), between the coordination (O(4)) and the lattice water molecules (O(5), between the lattice water molecules (O(5), O(6)) and the free triazole nitrogen atoms (N(8), N(9)). The crystal is stable with these hydrogen bonding interactions. Thermal analysis of complex 1 shows that the lattice and coordination water molecules are lost in a continuous fashion from 90 to 125°C (observed: 12.23%, calculated: 12.68%) (Fig. S5). The anhydrous [Ni3(ttmb)2Cl6] is stable up to 304°C. Then the complex discomposes continuously from 304 to 780°C (observed: 55.63%). Complex 2 exhibits the lattice and coordination water molecules are lost in a continuous fashion from 56 to 105°C (observed: 10.28%, calculated: 10.61%). The anhydrous [Ni(ttmb)2](NO3)2 is stable to 246°C, which is lower than that of complex 1 (304°C). Then the complex 2 discomposes continuously from 250 to 641°C and the residue substance is 21.25%. No reasonable fragments can be assigned corresponding to the weight loss process of complexes 1 and 2. Two Ni(II) coordination polymers [Ni3(ttmb)2(H2O)6Cl6](H2O)3 (1) and [Ni(ttmb)2(H2O)2](NO3)2(H2O)4 (2) were synthesized by the reaction of the flexible ligand 1,3,5-tri(1,2,4-triazol-1-ylmethyl)-2,4,6-trimethylbenzene (ttmb) with Ni(II) salts with different anions Cl− and NO 3 - . The structures are dependent on the anions. The Cl anions coordinate with Ni(II) atoms in complex 1, but the NO 3 - anions merely balance the charges of complex 2. The flexible ligand ttmb shows the cis,cis,cis-conformation and a tri-monodentate coordination mode in 1. However the ttmb ligand exhibits the cis,trans,trans-conformation and a two-monodentate coordination mode in 2. Complex 1 contains an undulated (3,4)-connected two-dimensional network. Each undulated 2D network polycatenates other two identical networks, thus giving a rare (2D→3D) parallel polycatenation network. However complex 2 has a two-dimensional (4,4) planar network. On the basis of this work, further syntheses and structural studies of novel coordination polymers with flexible tri(triazole) ligands are also under way in our laboratory.
