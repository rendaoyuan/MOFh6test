Manuscript Dalton Transactions PAPER Cite this: Dalton Trans., 2014, 43, 12528 Received 26th May 2014, Accepted 20th June 2014 DOI: 10.1039/c4dt01535e www.rsc.org/dalton Coligand-directed synthesis of ﬁve Co(II)/Ni(II) coordination polymers with a neutral tetradentate ligand: syntheses, crystal structures, and properties†‡ Ling Qin,a Zhong-Jie Wang,b Ting Wang,a He-Gen Zheng*a and Jin-Xi Chen*b The solvothermal reactions of 1,1’-oxybis[3,5-di-4-pyridine]-benzene (L) and transition metal cations (Co and Ni) aﬀord ﬁve novel coordination polymers in the presence of ﬂexible bridging ligands (4,4’-H2nba = 4,4’-dicarboxydiphenylamine, H2cam = D-camphoric acid, 4,4’-H2sdb = 4,4’-sulfonyldibenzoic acid, H2chdc = 1,4-trans-cyclohexanedicarboxylic acid), namely {[Co2L2(OH)2(nba)]·2DMF}n (1), {[CoL(cam)- (H2O)]}n (2), {[Co3(L)(4,4’-sdb)3(H2O)]·1.5CH3CN·4H2O}n (3), {[Ni3(L)(4,4’-sdb)3(H2O)]·1.5CH3CN·4H2O}n (4), and {[Ni2L2(chdc)2(H2O)2]·(H2O)3}n (5) (DMF = N,N-dimethylformamide). Their structures have been determined by single-crystal X-ray diﬀraction analyses and further characterized by elemental analyses, IR spectroscopy, and powder X-ray diﬀraction. Complex 1 reveals a 2-fold interpenetrating three-dimen- sional (3D) framework with the Schläﬂi symbol {4·8·104}{4·8·10} topology. Compound 2 crystallizes in the achiral space group with the D-camphorate ligand racemized. Compounds 3 and 4 reveal similar structure with the {3·44·6}{32·48·59·69} topology based on a linear trinuclear building block M3(OOCR)6 (M = Co(II) or Ni(II)). Compound 5 is a wavy sheet, where both carboxylate and L ligands act as bidentate ligands. Moreover, UV-Visible absorption spectra of complexes 1–3, 5 and the magnetic properties of 3 have been investigated. Introduction The design and synthesis of coordination polymers (CPs) have attracted increasing interest not only because of their appeal- ing structural and topological novelty but also owing to their tremendous potential applications as functional materials such as nonlinear optics,1 molecular magnetism,2 gas storage,3 drug delivery,4 ferroelectricity,5 sensors,6 and cataly- sis.7 Generally, the structural diversity of such crystalline materials is dependent on many factors, such as metal ions,8 templating agents,9 temperature,10 solvents,11 counteranions12 and organic ligands.13 Among the strategies, the rational selec- tion of organic ligands or coligands according to their length, rigidity, space and functional groups is important for the assembly of structural controllable coordinated polymers; based on this strategy, many significant studies have been performed.14 Recently, we reported a neutral tetradentate non-rigid ligand, 1,1′-oxybis[3,5-di-4-pyridine]-benzene (L) (Scheme 1).15 By introduction of diﬀerent co-ligands, 4,4′-dicarboxydiphenyl- amine (H2nba),16 D-camphoric acid (H2cam),17 4,4′-sulfonyl- dibenzoic acid (4,4′-H2sdb),18 and trans-1,4-cyclohexane- dicarboxylic acid (trans-H2chdc),19 a remarkable range of materials containing various architectures can be prepared, that is {[Co2L2(OH)2(nba)]·2DMF}n (1), {[CoL(cam)(H2O)]}n (2), {[Co3(L)(4,4′-sdb)3(H2O)]·1.5CH3CN·4H2O}n (3), {[Ni3(L)(4,4′- sdb)3(H2O)]·1.5CH3CN·4H2O}n (4), and {[Ni2L2(chdc)2(H2O)2]· (H2O)3}n (5). These compounds were synthesized under the same hydrothermal conditions. The coligand-directed syn- thesis has a great eﬀect on the final structures. C, H and N analyses were carried out with a Perkin Elmer 240C elemental analyzer. Powder X-ray diﬀraction (PXRD) measurements were performed using a Bruker D8 Advance X-ray diﬀractometer using Mo-Kα radiation (λ = 0.71073 Å), in which the X-ray tube was operated at 40 kV and 40 mA. Solid-state UV-vis diﬀuse reflectance spectra were obtained at room temperature using a Shimadzu UV-3600 double monochromator spectrophotometer, and BaSO4 was used as a 100% reflectance standard for all materials. Temp- erature dependent magnetic susceptibility data for polycrystal- line complex 3 were obtained using a SQUID magnetometer under an applied field of 2000 Oe over the temperature range of 1.8–300 K. Synthetic procedure: Compounds 1–5 can be synthesized by a general step: a mixture of metal nitrate, L and dicarboxylic acid was dissolved in a DMF–H2O–CH3CN mixed solvent. The final mixture was placed in a Parr Teflon-lined stainless steel vessel (25 mL) under autogenous pressure and heated at 95 °C for 3 days. The detailed information of synthesis and the main features are displayed in Table 1. X-Ray crystallography Single crystals of 1–5 were prepared by the methods described in the synthetic procedures. X-Ray crystallographic data of these compounds 1–5 were collected using a Bruker Apex Smart CCD diﬀractometer with graphite-monochromated Mo Kα radiation (λ = 0.71073 Å). Structure solutions were solved by direct methods and the non-hydrogen atoms were located from the trial structure and then refined anisotropi- cally with SHELXTL using full-matrix least-squares procedures based on F2 values. A semiempirical absorption correction was applied using SADABS.20 The hydrogen atom positions were fixed geometrically at calculated distances and allowed to ride on the parent atoms. The distributions of peaks in the chan- nels of 1, 3, and 4 were chemically featureless to refine using conventional discrete-atom models. To resolve these issues, the contribution of the electron density by the remaining solvent molecules was removed using the SQUEEZE routine in PLATON.21 The relevant crystallographic data are presented in Table 2, while the selected bond lengths and angles are given in the ESI (Table S1‡). View Article Online Results and discussion Crystal structure of {[Co2L2(OH)2(nba)]·2DMF}n (1) Compound 1 crystallizes in the tetragonal crystal system in the space group I41/acd (no. 142). The asymmetric unit consists of two L ligands, one nba2−ligand, and two OH−anions. Each Co(II) ion exhibits a distorted octahedral configuration co- ordinated by two oxygen atoms from one chelating carboxylate group, three nitrogen atoms from the L ligand and one OH− anion (Fig. 1a). Both Co–O (2.046(2)–2.162(2) Å) and Co–N (2.109(2)–2.219(2) Å) bond lengths are in the normal range reported for cobalt complexes.22 Each L ligand links three Co(II) ions resulting in a 2D layer as shown in Fig. 1b, the fourth pyridine group does not involve in coordination. The nba2−ligands connect the 2D layers to form a three dimensional net (Fig. 1c). A better insight into the nature of this intricate framework is provided using the TOPOS analysis software,23 reducing multidimensional struc- tures to simple nodes and connection nets. The L ligands and metal cations can be regarded as a 3 and 4-connected node, respectively. Therefore, the whole structure can be represented as a 3,4-connected topology net with the Schläfli symbol {4·8·104}{4·8·10} (Fig. 1d). The nets are mutually interpene- trated to generate the 2-fold interpenetration (Fig. 1e). Crystal structure of {[CoL(cam)(H2O)]}n (2) D-Camphoric acid is a cyclopentane derivative with two carboxylate groups functionalized on the 1,3-carbon atoms, of which 1-carbon atom has a further methyl group, and two methyls occupy at the 2-carbon atom, therefore, being an enantiopure ditopic organic linker. Compound 2 crystallizes in the achiral space group with the chiral D-camphorate ligand racemized. The asymmetry unit of 2 contains one Co(II) atom, one L, one cam2−ligand and two water molecules. As depicted in Fig. 2a, the camphorate ligands are always inherently dis- ordered over two positions. Each Co(II) atom is six-coordinated to two pyridyl nitrogen atoms from L ligands, three oxygen atoms of cam2−ligands, and one water molecule giving a dis- torted [CoN2O4] octahedral geometry. The equatorial plane is completed by the coordination of N2, O2, O3, and O4 atoms. The axial positions are occupied by N1 and O1w atoms with a N1–Co1–O1w bond angle of 178.77(13)°. The Co–O bond dis- tances vary from 2.051(3) to 2.230(3) Å, and the average Co–N bond distance is 2.118 Å. The D-H2cam is completely deprotonated. The cam2−ligands coordinate to Co1 ions via a chelating carboxyl group and a monodentate carboxyl group resulting in the formation of a 1D chain, which is connected by the L ligand to form a 2D layer, as shown in Fig. 2b. Crystal structures of {[Co3(L)(4,4′-sdb)3(H2O)]·1.5CH3CN· 4H2O}n (3) and {[Ni3(L)(4,4′-sdb)3(H2O)]·1.5CH3CN·4H2O}n (4) The X-ray crystallographic analysis reveals that compounds 3 and 4 have similar structures. Thus only the structure of 4 is discussed here in detail. There are three crystallographically independent Ni(II) ions in 4 as shown in Fig. 3a. Ni1 is co- ordinated in an octahedral geometry by four carboxylate oxygen atoms from three 4,4′-sdb anions and two nitrogen atoms from two L molecules on the axial positions. Ni3 is co- ordinated by five 4,4′-sdb anions and one water oxygen. However, Ni2 is coordinated in a tetragonal pyramid geometry by three carboxylate groups of three 4,4′-sdb anions and two nitrogen atoms from two L molecules. Such a building block is further linked to eight neighboring trinuclear units, leading to a three-dimensional framework (Fig. 3b). The overall structure of the 3D network can be described as a 4,8-connected net (Fig. 3c), which can be further specified by the Schläfli symbol {3·44·6}{32·48·59·69}. Crystal structure of {[Ni2L2(chdc)2(H2O)2]·(H2O)3}n (5) Single-crystal X-ray diﬀraction analysis reveals that compound 5 crystallizes in the monoclinic space group P1ˉ. The asym- metric unit contained one Ni2+ center connected to the L ligand, one chdc2−ligand, and one coordinated water mole- cule together with guest water molecules (Fig. 4a). Each Ni(II) ion shows a distorted octahedral configuration coordinated by three oxygen atoms from one chelating and one monodentate carboxylate groups, two nitrogen atoms from L ligands and one water molecule. As shown in Fig. 4b, both carboxylate and L ligands act as bidentate ligands combined with Ni atoms to form a wavy sheet (Fig. 4b). When considering the H-bond interaction (O6–H2W⋯N4), one of the uncoordinated N atoms of the A layer interacts with the uncoordinated carboxylate oxygen atoms of the B layer to form a rare binodal 3,5-co- ordinated 3D architecture with a {63}{69·8} hms topology (Fig. 4c). The nets are mutually interpenetrated to generate the 2-fold interpenetration (Fig. 4d). Magnetic properties Temperature-dependent magnetic susceptibility measure- ments for compound 3 were performed on the polycrystalline sample in the temperature range of 1.8–300 K in a fixed mag- netic field of 2 kOe, and the magnetic behaviours in the form of χMT and χM versus T plots are depicted in Fig. 5. Direct- current (dc) magnetic susceptibility studies reveal a room- temperature χMT value of 8.14 cm3 K mol−1, larger than that for three free S = 3/2 spins (χMT = 5.625 cm3 K mol−1 and g = 2) for three high-spin Co(II) atoms in each Co3 unit per formula. The larger experimental value than the corresponding spin only value can be assigned to the unquenched orbital-moment as a result of spin–orbit coupling in the distorted octahedral coordinated Co(II) ions.24 The χMT product gradually decreases with lowering temperature, reaching a value of 7.44 cm3 K mol−1 at 100 K, then decreases quickly to a minimum value of 4.3 cm3 K mol−1 at 1.8 K. The result at the high temperature region reveals the antiferromagnetic interaction between the neighboring Co(II) ions. The decrease at low temperature was due mainly to the antiferromagnetic coupling interaction between the neighboring Co(II) ions and contribution of the orbital momentum of the single Co(II) ion. The magnetic sus- ceptibilities obey the Curie–Weiss law in the temperature range above 30 K, giving a negative Weiss constant θ = −14.79 K and Curie constant C = 8.57 emu K mol−1. View Article Online UV-Visible spectra and PXRD analyses As can be seen in the UV-vis absorbance spectra (Fig. S5‡), the L ligand shows intense absorption peaks respectively at 250–290 nm, which can be ascribed to π–π* transitions of the ligands. Compounds 1–3 show two broad bands in the UV range at 350–400 nm and in the visible region at 450–550 nm. The higher energy bands (in the UV range) are assigned to metal-to-ligand charge transfer (MLCT) transitions, while the lower energy bands arise from d–d transitions. In the visible region, we observe one additional peak at 460 nm [4T1g(F)→ 4T2g(F)] with a shoulder at 550 nm [4T1g(F) →4T1g(P)] tran- sitions. Besides, one additional peak is observed for Ni(II) com- pound 5, which appeared at 630 nm [3T1g(F) →3A2g].25 Phase purity of the bulk materials was confirmed by comparison of their powder diﬀraction (PXRD) patterns with those calculated from single-crystal X-ray diﬀraction studies (Fig. S6–S9‡). Conclusion In summary, we successfully synthesized five coordination polymers, which were constructed from 1,1′-oxybis[3,5-di-4-pyri- dine]-benzene in the presence of diﬀerent carboxylic acids co- ligands under the same hydrothermal conditions. Complex 1 reveals a 2-fold interpenetrating 3D net where three nitrogen atoms of L ligands were involved in coordination. Complexes 2 and 5 have similar structures, and feature a 2D undulated (4,4)-layered structure in which only two nitrogen atoms of L ligands involved in coordination.
