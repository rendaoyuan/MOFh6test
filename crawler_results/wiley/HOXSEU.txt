Four 3D lanthanide organic frameworks from potassium pyrazine-2,3,5,6-tetracarboxylate (K4pztc) or potassium pyridine- 2,3,5,6-tetracarboxylate (K4pdtc), namely, {[KEu(pztc)(H2O)2]·H2O}n (1), {[KTb(pztc)(H2O)2]·1.25H2O}n (2), {[KLn(pdtc)(H2O)]·H2O}n [Ln = Gd (3), Ho (4)], were synthesized by reaction of the correspond- ing lanthanide oxides with K4pztc or K4pdtc in presence of HCl under hydrothermal conditions, and characterized by elemental analysis, 1 Introduction Lanthanide elements are well-known as important candi- dates to construct metal-organic frameworks (MOFs) because of their ability to form multifunctional materials with the inter- esting magnetic, optical, and catalytic properties.[1] Moreover, lanthanide elements have higher coordination numbers and more flexible coordination geometry, which are helpful in the formation of unusual MOFs.[2] On the other hand, depending on the instinctive high oxophilicity (hard acid-hard base inter- action),[3] lanthanide ions are strongly coordinated by oxygen donor ligands. Hence, ligands with carboxylate groups are ex- pected to be good organic ligands in constructing lanthanide- organic hybrid networks.[4] Pyrazine-2,3,5,6-tetracarboxylic acid (H4pztc) and pyridine- 2,3,5,6-tetracarboxylic acid (H4pdtc) are suitable ligands for the construction of MOFs due to the presence of nitrogen and oxygen atoms.[5] Furthermore, compared with the correspond- ing di-, tri-carboxylic acids, they contain richer coordination sites and are more symmetrical. Thus, it is aimed to obtain more fascinating and richer structures by the self-assembly be- tween H4pdtc/H4pztc and lanthanide metals. * Dr. A.-H. In complexes 1 and 2, the dodecadentate chelator pztc4– links four LnIII ions and four KI ions. The coordination mode of the pztc4– ligand is reported for the first time herein. Complexes 3 and 4 are isostructural with earlier reported Nd, Dy, Er complexes. Moreover, the EuIII and TbIII complexes exhibit the characteristic luminescence. However, metal-organic frameworks containing lanthanide are far less studied[6] than d-block elements because the unspe- cific coordination properties of the lanthanide ions render the design of lanthanide-based coordination frameworks with spe- cific properties very challenging. Some 2D/3D MOFs from po- lycarboxylate ligands and lanthanide metals have been pre- pared[7] through taking certain factors into account, such as (1) the coordination nature of the metal ions; (2) the shape, functionality, flexibility, symmetry of the organic ligand; (3) different synthesis methods, solvent, conditions (pH tempera- ture) etc. They usually adopt diverse binding modes as multi- dentate chelators to obtain interesting polymeric forms. They have similar structure except for the numbers of uncoordinated water molecules (2 has 0.25 uncoor- dinated water molecule more than 1). So, complex 1 is taken as an example to introduce the coordination structure. All the carboxyl groups of the ligand are deprotonated, and show rich coordinated modes, whereas each pdtc4– participate coordination through a tridecadentate mode (Figures S6 and S7, Supporting Information). Lanthanide contraction effect can be observed through com- paring the average Ln–N and Ln–O bond lengths among the reported three complexes and 3 and 4 (Table 2). Selected bond lengths and angles for complexes 3 and 4 are listed in Tables S3 and S4 (Supporting Information). Like the other three com- plexes, KI ions in 3 and 4 also participate in coordination, which makes the structures of 3 and 4 much richer. The coordi- nated environments of KI ions in complexes 3 and 4 are dif- ferent (Figure S8, Supporting Information). In 3, KI ion is seven-coordinate and adopts the same coordination mode as in the NdIII complex. In 4, KI is eight-coordinate and adopts the same coordination mode as in the DyIII complex. Owing to the 3D Lanthanide Organic Frameworks with N-Heterocyclic Polycarboxylic Acids role of KI ions, the 3D organic frameworks appear much richer and fascinating (Figure S9, Supporting Information). The structures of 1 and 2 are very similar, therefore only the TGA of 2 was operated in a nitrogen atmosphere in the temperature range of 30 to 750 °C. For 2, the first weight loss below 135 °C is 5.04%, corresponding to the loss of 1.25 uncoordinated water mo- lecules (calcd. 4.43%). The second weight loss, 8.15%, oc- curred from 135 to 300 °C, which was due to the loss of two coordinated water molecules (calcd. 7.08%). After the loss of water molecules, a continuous weight loss occurred in the tem- perature range 300–705 °C, which was due to the degradation of pztc4– ligand. Above 705 °C, terbium oxide and K2CO3 were obtained representing the remaining mass of 49.78% (calcd. 46.01%). The structures of 3 and 4 are similar. Compound 3 is taken as an example to study the thermal behavior in an atmosphere of air in the temperature range of 30 to 750 °C. For 3, the first weight loss from 70–170 °C is 4.53%, corresponding to the loss of one uncoordinated water molecule (calcd. 3.73%). The second weight loss, 3.87%, occurred from 170 to 320 °C, which was due to the loss of one coordinated water molecule (calcd. 3.73%). From 320 to 680 °C, the weight loss was due to the degradation of pdtc4– ligand. Above 680 °C, gadolinium oxide and K2CO3 were obtained representing the remaining mass of 48.18% (calcd. 51.78%). 3.4 Fluorescent Properties The fluorescent spectra for complexes 1 and 2 are measured at room temperature in aqueous solution with the concentration of 10–5 mol·L–1. Complexes 1 and 2 exhibit the corresponding characteristic luminescence when the excitation wavelengths are 350 nm and 330 nm, respectively. The emitting spectra of 1 and 2 are shown in Figure 5. The fluorescent spectrum of 1 exhibits the characteristic transition of the EuIII ion. The peaks at 570, 591, 615, 649, and 692 nm are attributed 5D0 to 7FJ (J = 0, 1, 2, 3, 4) transitions. It is well-known that the 5D0 to 7F0 transition is strictly forbidden by symmetry.[12] As the 5D0 to 7F0 transition is observed, the spectra reveal that the EuIII ion does not lie in a centrosymmetric coordination site, which agrees with the result of X-ray single-crystal diffraction. Ligands pztc4– and pdtc4– coordinate to s-block and f- block metals by dodecadentate and tridecadentate chelating modes, respectively. Owing to the participation of KI ions, complexes 1–4 show much richer frameworks. In 1 and 2, a porous, nano-sized channel with 11.7% porosity occupied by free water molecules were observed. Furthermore, the coordi- nation mode of the pztc4– ligand is reported for the first time. EuIII and TbIII complexes show characteristic luminescence emission in the visible region.
