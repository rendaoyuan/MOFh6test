John Plater,(1)∗Mark R.St.J. Foreman,(1) Benjamin M. De Silva,(1) Janet M.S. Skakle,(1) and Alexandra M.Z. Slawin(2) Received May 21, 2001 The synthesis and molecular structure of the title compound is reported. There are two different Cu atoms in separate chains which are linked to form double-stranded chains. Each Cu has a distorted octahedral coordination geometry. The structure has a triclinic space group P-1 with a = 11.0764(5), b = 15.9276(7), c = 18.2000(7) ˚A, V = 3197.9(2) ˚A3, and Z = 2. KEY WORDS: Copper; crystallization; amide bond; crystal engineering. Introduction Our research has focussed on the crys- tallization of different bipyridyl ligands with metal-ions to give coordination networks. Lig- ands connected by a ﬂexible spacer have proved to be useful for preparing networks with unusual modes of interpenetration.1,2 Recently bipyridyl ligands connected by amide functionality have been prepared and used for the formation of novel coordination networks.3 The amide func- tional group could form inter- or intrastrand hy- drogen bonds and so direct the nature of the network that crystallizes. In the case of the ligand N,N′-bis(4-pyridylmethyl)isophthalamide crystallization with copper nitrate gave a novel structure which contained a water decamer (H2O)10 with an ice like molecular arrangement.4 (1) Department of Chemistry, University of Aberdeen, Meston Walk, Aberdeen AB24 3UE, United Kingdom. Discreet water clusters are of structural and theo- retical interest.5 We are studying the formation of a range of coordination networks from bipyridyl ligands with hydrogen bond donor and acceptor spacers and metal-ions in the presence and ab- sence of 2,2-bipyridyl to investigate the inﬂuence of hydrogen bonding upon network structure and properties. For structure 1, selected bond lengths and an- gles are given in Table 1; hydrogen bond lengths and angles are given in Table 2; and key crystal- lographic data is summarized in Table 3. Table 4 has atomic coordinates and equivalent isotropic displacement parameters. There are two different Cu atoms in the structure. Cu1 has a distorted oc- tahedral coordination geometry consisting of four pyridine nitrogens in equatorial sites and two wa- ter oxygens in the axial sites (Fig. 1). The rings of the two pyridines from the bridging ligands are arranged such that the plane formed by the rings is not parallel with the plane of the atoms in the 2,2′-bipridyl ligand. Like Cu1 Cu2 has a distorted octahedral coordination geometry.
